  • Kyiv Pechersk Lavra

    01.10.2018 by Ivan Sidorenko

    One of the largest and most famous Orthodox monasteries, located in the capital of Ukraine, Kyiv. It is one of the most important religious and historical sites not only in Ukraine but in the world. The Lavra is a UNESCO World Heritage site due to its architectural and cultural significance.

  • Bukovel and Hoverla

    11.02.2014 by Sara Dironenko

    Hoverla, at 2,061 meters, is the highest peak in Ukraine and a popular destination in the Carpathian Mountains. Nearby, Bukovel is a top-tier ski resort, attracting visitors with its modern facilities and picturesque surroundings.

  • Maidan Nezalezhnosti

    27.12.2017 by Diner Ivanov

    Maidan Nezalezhnosti (Independence Square) is the central and most significant public square in Kyiv, the capital of Ukraine. It is widely regarded as the heart of the city, both geographically and symbolically, representing the nation's journey toward independence and freedom.

  • Kyiv Pechersk Lavra

    Kyiv Pechersk Lavra was founded in the late 11th century, around 1051, by monks Antony and Theodosius. Initially, it was a cave monastery where the monks lived in underground caves they had dug themselves. The site quickly became popular among pilgrims due to its holy relics and spiritual life. The Lavra consists of several grand churches, monastery buildings, and magnificent architectural complexes.

  • Maidan Nezalezhnosti

    The Orange Revolution (2004) was a mass protest against electoral fraud, leading to a rerun of the presidential election and Viktor Yushchenko's victory. A decade later, the Euromaidan protests (2013-2014) began over Ukraine's rejection of an EU agreement, evolving into a nationwide movement for democracy and reform. The Revolution of Dignity marked a turning point, with the ousting of President Yanukovych and a shift towards a European path, as Maidan became a symbol of resistance and hope.
